Understanding Criminal Laws

Knowledge is power. Understand the laws that may affect your case with our comprehensive guide.

Common Criminal Offenses

Assault and Battery

Defined as intentionally causing harmful or offensive contact with another person, or the threat to do so. Penalties vary by severity and circumstances.

Drug Possession

Controlled Substances Act regulates possession of drugs classified by schedule. Penalties depend on type, amount, and prior offenses.

Theft and Larceny

Unlawful taking of someone else's property with intent to permanently deprive them of it. Values determine whether charged as misdemeanor or felony.

Fraud

Intentional deception for unlawful gain, including identity theft, credit card fraud, insurance fraud, and more.

The Criminal Process

1. Arrest and Booking

After arrest, the defendant is processed at the police station. This includes fingerprinting, photographing, and recording personal information.

2. Bail Hearing

A judge determines if the defendant can be released on bail and sets the amount based on flight risk and danger to community.

3. Arraignment

Defendant appears before judge to hear charges and enter a plea (guilty, not guilty, or no contest).

4. Pre-Trial Motions

Attorneys may file motions to suppress evidence or dismiss charges based on legal arguments.

5. Trial

The prosecution must prove guilt beyond reasonable doubt. Defendant may choose jury or bench trial.

6. Sentencing

If convicted, judge determines sentence based on guidelines, nature of crime, and defendant's history.
